About Us

Hello there. One of us is Jess, and the other one of us is Mark. We're nerds who play traditional (kind of) music together. Jess is best known right now for playing in contra dance band Pepperjack, and Mark is best known for his incredible tune-writing hax. Our music has grown together over the last decade and so here's a site for all that.

ORIGINAL TUNES

Here's a bunch of tunes we wrote. Hover over a tune for more info.

Avery's Jig

Jig

D dor

An early Mark jig written for a roleplay character, who rescued a fiddler from a predicament and received a tune in return. April 2013.

Behind the Waterfall

Reel

D dor/mix

Jess wrote this one. The name comes from the sound of agonizing and patient practice, best captured by Makar from the Wind Waker (Zelda 2003), who can be heard scratching out an eternal practice session behind a waterfall. May 2017.

Sheet music (.pdf)

Delilah & Me

Slow reel

G maj

A tune to remember the service dog of a friend and all around Good Girl named Delilah. May 2018.

Sheet music (.pdf)

February Summer

Waltz

D mix

We collaborated on this one to commemorate our friend Derry's birthday, during a February filled with summery days playing music outside. We ended up with a mildly Scandinavian sounding mixolydian waltz. February 2018.

Sheet music (.pdf)

Kittelish

Jig

A mix

A 3-part jig, the C part of which came to Mark when he was in a lab meeting. Named because that part sounds just complicated enough and with a sweeping enough chord progression that it reminds us of something Jeremey Kittel might play. July 2017.

Sheet music (.pdf)

Leggy Boy Bat

Jig

D maj

A 4-part jig named after another Good Dog. The tune is too long because his legs are too long. June 2018.

Made by the Sea

Reel

E mix

Mark wrote this because he was inspired by a legendary stream of Final Fantasy 7 by Shaun and Jen in which Jen asserted her beliefs on how shells are made. March 2018.

Meeting of the Minds

Reel

G mix

We wrote this together after a long visit by Mark to the Bay Area to commemorate a real nice and productive time together. As pointed out by Jess' bandmate Scott, the ending leads into "Get Back." Have fun! May 2018.

No Sleep Tonight

Reel

F# min

Mark wrote this one because his sleeping habits are terrible. Fondly remembering nights climbing into bed while the birds start screaming outside before dawn. A bit hard to tell whether it's actually F# minor or A major. Either way, starts on a D chord. Nov 2017.

Spider Jive

Mark and Derry (from Pepperjack) collaborated on this for Jess's birthday. Somehow it turned into a Halloween tune. Named by Jess for its Undertale vibes. May 2018.

The Shot in the Dark

Jig

D dor

A twisty jig named after the ship that our D&D party sailed in during a particularly long arc at sea in which many friends and enemies were made. February 2018.

Written in honor of Jess's D&D character, Chaszwik. Named after his frankly overpowered ability which allows him to roll about 70 damage dice on every attack. Chaszwik rulez ok. Nov 2016.

Tune from Under a Rock

Reel

G maj

Mark wrote this on Christmas Eve. Named for a tune which is well known on the fairy island of Emain Abla (yeah we're talking about D&D again), and often played by a dryad flautist named Felim. Dec 2017.

Reel

G min

In commemoration of competitive Guild Wars 2 thief player Sindrener's ability to see an enemy thief's intent to steal in his eyes. One of the scarier sounding tunes here. "I listened to it." - Sindrener. May 2017.
