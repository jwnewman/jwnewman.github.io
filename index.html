About Us

Hello there. One of us is Jess, and the other one of us is Mark. We play traditional (kind of) music together. We're also in other bands, separately. Here is our music stuff for all that. All the Jess and/or Mark content you crave. Enjoy yourselves.

ORIGINAL TUNES

Here's a bunch of tunes we wrote. Hover over a tune for more info.

Avery's Jig

Jig

D dor

An early Mark jig written for a roleplay character, who rescued a fiddler from a predicament and received a tune in return. April 2013.

Sheet music (.pdf)

Behind the Waterfall

Reel

D dor/mix

Jess wrote this one. The name comes from the sound of agonizing practice over something that is not at all under your fingers, best captured by Makar from the Wind Waker (Zelda 2003), who can be heard scratching out a practice session behind a waterfall. May 2017.

Sheet music (.pdf)

Delilah & Me

Slow reel

G major

A tune to remember the service dog of a friend and all around Good Girl named Delilah. May 2018.

Sheet music (.pdf)

February Summer

Waltz

D mix

We collaborated on this one to commemorate our friend Derry's birthday. We ended up with a mildly Scandinavian sounding mixolydian waltz. February 2018.

Sheet music (.pdf)

Kittelish

Jig

A mix

A 3-part jig, the C part of which came to Mark when he was in a lab meeting. Named because that part sounds just complicated enough and with a sweeping enough chord progression that it reminds us of something Jeremey Kittel might play. July 2017.

Sheet music (.pdf)

Leggy Boy Bat

Jig

D maj

A 4-part jig named after another Good Dog. The tune is too long because his legs are too long. June 2018.

Sheet music (.pdf)

Made by the Sea

Reel

E mix

Mark wrote this because he was inspired by a legendary stream of Final Fantasy 7 by Shaun and Jen in which Jen asserted her beliefs on how shells are made. March 2018.

Sheet music (.pdf)

Meeting of the Minds

Reel

G mix

We wrote this together after a long visit by Mark to the Bay Area to commemorate a real nice and productive time together. May 2018.

Sheet music (.pdf)

No Sleep Tonight

Reel

F# min

Mark wrote this one because his sleeping habits are terrible. Fondly remembering nights climbing into bed while the birds start screaming outside before dawn. A bit hard to tell whether it's actually F# minor or A major. Either way, starts on a D chord. Nov 2017.
